  <name>Task 2: Fix black bar animation on sheet full expansion</name>
  <files>Travel app/Views/Map/TripMapView.swift, Travel app/Views/Map/MapBottomSheet.swift</files>
  <action>
The black bar at the top when search is focused comes from the NavigationStack's toolbar area. When the bottom sheet expands to `.full`, it pushes content up into the navigation bar region. The `.toolbarBackground(.hidden, for: .navigationBar)` hides the toolbar background, but during the sheet animation the system briefly shows a dark region.

**TripMapView.swift:**
- Add `.toolbarColorScheme(.dark, for: .navigationBar)` alongside the existing `.toolbarBackground(.hidden, for: .navigationBar)` — this ensures any transient toolbar chrome is transparent/light, not black
- If the black bar persists, the issue is the MapBottomSheet going to `screenHeight` (full) which overlaps the safe area. The sheet's `.full` case returns `screenHeight` but the sheet's VStack uses `Spacer(minLength: 0)` at top, which means the sheet content starts at the very top of the screen including under the status bar area.

**MapBottomSheet.swift — safe area handling for full mode:**
- In the `.full` case height calculation (line 9-14), subtract safe area top inset so the sheet stops just below the status bar instead of going under it
- Change `case .full: return screenHeight` to `case .full: return screenHeight - geo.safeAreaInsets.top` by passing safe area insets from the GeometryReader
- Alternative simpler fix: in the VStack containing sheet content (line 45), add `.padding(.top, detent == .full ? geo.safeAreaInsets.top : 0)` so that in full mode the drag handle doesn't go under the status bar

Pick the approach that eliminates the black bar. The goal is: when sheet goes to full, no dark/black region appears between the status bar and the sheet content.
  </action>
